Scratch

Lesson 4: Pong and Physics Exercise

Introduction to Challenge

In comparison to the other tutorials, constructing the classic game pong should not be a challenge. The basis of pong is a lot like ping pong and brick breaker. In pong, two players lobby a ball back and forth. If the ball, touches the players respective edge, then -the opponent scores a point. The first player to ten wins.

This exercise will assess your understanding of the scratch interface and your scratch logic. If you understood the logic behind the previous exercises, you should complete this challenge in 15 minutes or less.

Instructions

Construct and program paddles for two players Construct the ball and physical behavior of the bounces
Construct a scoring system Construct winning and losing scenarios

Course Conclusion

Congratulations! You have finished our Scratch course. Within this course, you learned some of the basics of computer logic. Let’s backtrack which concepts you’ve learned. You learned how to do if/then statements, and how to create scoring systems through messaging, broadcasting, and sensing. You were able to construct relationships through objects interacting.
